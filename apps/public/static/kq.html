All data on this page is compiled using real tweets. Tweet data doesn't tell the future and it's not always 100% accurate. This site and its creator are not affiliated with Kanye West in any way. This site and its data are meant for editorial purposes only.

But what does it all mean?

In the simplest terms, we are measuring how people feel in a tweet where the word "Kanye" is mentioned. We use a process called sentiment analysis in which our software reads each tweet and determines levels of positivity, negativity, and neutrality. The dominant emotion is then recorded and indexed every hour.

The chart on this site represents the dominantely postive tweets vs the dominant negative tweets by the hour for the last 24 hours.

How is location determined?

Twitter provides a general location "range" on a per tweet basis. Most tweets do not have location data and therefore can not be represented in the location based data on this site.

Why?

In light of Kanye West’s recent VMA announcement of his 2020 Presidential bid, kanyequest.com seeks to measure twitter responses to the candidate over the years leading up to the election.

Created by Little Rock based design and development firm Few, Kanye Quest tracks and compiles the positive or negative aspect of all tweets related to to Kanye.

As we have witnessed with Obama’s presidential victories, future elections will play out in large part on the virtual stage of social media, a place where Yeezy and wife Kim Kardashian already reign supreme.

If Yeezy wishes to gauge his preliminary approval rating, he need look no further: Kanye Quest offers a visual representation of Yeezy’s approval in the Twittersphere via dynamic infographics of twitter analytics including wordclouds, sentiment analysis, and quantitative and regional data.
